I decided to take a impromptu vacation day today from work and headed out to Sunrise for a little me time in the outdoors. When I arrived at the parking lot at Sunrise (8:45am) I was pleasantly surprised to find just a handful of cars here.

After putting my boots on and gathering my things, I was working my way up the Sourdough Ridge trail towards Frozen Lake by 9:00am and encountered a few packs of people as I my way west. As I neared Frozen lake, I noticed a number of hikers making their way up towards Bouroughs, so I changed my plan and headed towards Skyscraper Mountain instead.

As I started working my way down the Wonderland Trail, a bit of a breeze picked up, which not only felt great, but helped keep the bugs away. This made for perfect conditions to be outside and the trail itself was also in very good shape. The best part is that all the snow fields have melted away, which makes life a lot easier as well.

I made my way along the Wonderland, past Berkley Park and up the ridge to Skyscraper Pass. Veer right as you work your way down to the path that leads up Skyscraper to the summit and call it good.

Note: The WTA hiking info calls the last climb to the top a scramble. While I wouldn't exactly call it a scramble because I never once had to use my hands to obtain the summit, there are several large steps needed to reach the top. Other than that, it's a series of short switchbacks on a loose gravel and soft dirt path up the spine, so here's where I'll give a recommendation. If you're going to the top to enjoy the breathtaking 360 view, wear proper footwear for the outdoors (something with good support and grip) and trekking poles. Since you'll not be scrambling with your hands, these two items will assist you with your balance as you not only make your way up, but down. Remember, you'll be going downhill on loose gravel at points.

I encountered two other hikers on my way out, and as expected, several more on the return trip back to Sunrise (but nothing like you'll see when you come out on a weekend day.)

As far as wildlife goes, I encountered marmont, pika and chipmunk's, but no goat or bear, so you can't win them all. :)

When I arrived back to the parking lot, I found it to be full and noticed cars circling, looking for spots, so it took 4 hours on the weekday to fill up.

Overall, I hiked 9 miles roundtrip and had a wonderful day enjoying the views along the way. The next time you're out at Sunrise and are looking for something to do that takes you away from the more popular Burroughs or Fremont hikes, consider a trek along the Wonderland trail out to Skyscraper instead.

Briefly, since there have been no new TR’s for a while, here’s a quick report:

Spent two days and a night up near the Portals. Lots of snow. Used boots and poles mostly, but on a couple of occasions in the off trail areas added micro-spikes and an axe. For the main Ptarmigan Ridge trail the worst spot (in my opinion) comes right after the split from the Chain Lakes trail. The PtRT drops a couple hundred feet to get below a rock outcrop and you’ll have to traverse a long-lived snow field, which is quite steep and can be hard in the early hours. If you get past that without trouble the rest of the snow crossings should be manageable, though extensive. The plateau area to the south of Kiser (sometimes called 14 Goat Lake or something similar) is still mostly full of snow, though there are some good tent sites melted out. Snow-wise, the route to the top of the East Portal is no more difficult than the rest of the trail.

Saw many, many, many goats. The goats up at Baker are nicely wary of people and don’t follow you around or eat your socks or that kind of thing.

Bugs were pretty bad Tuesday but settled down Wednesday when temperatures dropped a bit. Biting flies, biting gnats during the day. At dawn and dusk: hordes of mosquitoes.

Probably because of the snow there weren’t too many people up on Ptarmigan. By comparison Chain Lakes trail and especially Table Mountain were very busy, even on a Wednesday. The (vast) Artist Point parking lot was mostly full at 2pm Wednesday.
